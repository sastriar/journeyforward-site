How he got here

In 1971, Kwame packed one suitcase — three shirts, his certificates, and his mother's groundnut soup recipe, folded four times at a bus station in Kumasi. He had never been on a plane. He arrived in London and watched his breath appear in the cold air for the first time in his life. That recipe is now framed in his kitchen in Atlanta.

"My mother used to say: you carry the compound with you wherever you go. She was right. I have never been anywhere that didn't feel like I was trying to rebuild what we had on that street in Kumasi."

— Kwame Osei-Mensah · Recorded age 74 · Atlanta, 2022

"When my daughter got into medical school, my father said: 'Your grandmother carried cocoa on her back so you could have a back that only ever carries your own children.' I think about that every single day."

— Ama Osei-Mensah · Physician · Atlanta, 2023
